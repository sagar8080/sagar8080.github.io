29:["$","h2",null,{"children":"Decision 3: rebalancing"}]
2a:["$","p",null,{"children":"Eventually a node fills up, a node dies, or you add capacity and want the cluster to use it. Two schemes:"}]
2b:["$","p",null,{"children":[["$","strong",null,{"children":"Fixed partitions."}]," Pre-create more partitions than you have nodes. Say, 1024 partitions across 4 nodes. When a fifth node joins, it takes 256 partitions over from the existing four. Data moves once per topology change and predictably. The downside is you have to size the partition count up front; too few and you can't scale, too many and metadata overhead bites."]}]
2c:["$","p",null,{"children":[["$","strong",null,{"children":"Dynamic partitioning."}]," Partitions split when they grow past a threshold and merge when they shrink. You don't pre-size anything.
